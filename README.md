## üìä Key Findings

Based on the descriptive analysis of the 891 passengers in the dataset, the following insights were identified:

### 1. The Survival Hierarchy: Gender & Class
The data confirms that while the overall survival rate was **38.38%**, survival was heavily dictated by a combination of gender and social class. Women across all classes had a significantly higher probability of surviving than men. However, the "Class Gap" remains undeniable: a **First-Class** passenger was nearly **twice as likely to survive** as a **Third-Class** passenger.

### 2. The Critical Point: Vulnerability of Third-Class Men
The most striking finding is the extreme vulnerability of men in the lower socioeconomic bracket. **33.67% of the total passenger manifest** consisted of Third-Class men who did not survive. This group represents the single greatest human loss of the shipwreck, highlighting how the lack of privilege combined with gender expectations resulted in a tragic outcome.

### 3. Mortality Concentration
When analyzing the "Not Survived" group (which accounts for **61.62%** of the total passengers), the disparity is stark:
* **85.25%** of those who perished were men.
* Only **14.75%** were women. 

This reinforces that gender was the strongest determining factor for survival, although socioeconomic status significantly influenced the odds within each group.
